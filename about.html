I'm David Joerg, I'm a software developer, designer and entrepreneur in New York City.

I co-founded Vindigo in 1999. It was like Yelp, but the database was completely on the device since the wireless internet didn't work yet. I built advanced synchronization and compression technology to make it possible; the result was a magical zero-latency experience that attracted several hundred thousand rabidly loyal users.

Then I joined the founding team of Two Sigma Investments, which I helped build into a leading investment management company, now with over $20 billion in assets. I managed and grew a team of developers that built and operated our in-house software. We were very successful in a highly competitive field. In general, these kind of systems are very demanding in terms of uptime, speed, adaptability and instrumentation. I also ran a research department that studied how to improve the way we trade. We were doing Big Data before anybody called it that.

While I remain a Managing Director at Two Sigma, these days my work is primarily on interests outside of finance, in the areas of:

  • teaching kids to program
  • improving how people learn skills, especially game skils
  • strategy games such as Chess and StarCraft
  • helping people make better data-driven decisions

My latest project is GGTracker, a site for StarCraft players to get better faster by helping them analyze their games and their performance. It was my first high-end Web project; I learned all kinds of web technologies while doing it, and re-discovered my love for designing products.

I regularly advise entrepreneurs and startup companies in the burgeoning New York City technology scene.

I graduated from Harvard with a degree in computer science. My senior thesis was a visual programming syntax for Prolog.
